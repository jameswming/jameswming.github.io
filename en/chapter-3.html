Day twenty-seven. Or day seventy-two. David couldn't tell anymore.

He swallowed a mouthful of synthetic nutrient paste. Nothing on his tongue. He knew it was food... But he didn't remember that food should have taste.

He had already burned taste.

"System notice: Emotional mass remaining 47%," the AI said. "Estimated arrival at Singularity horizon in thirty cycles."

"Do you know why the Bureau chose you?" Su Qing asked.

"Because they calculated that before you reached the Singularity, you'd burn away all 'excess' memories. You'd become a perfect, uncontaminated——"

"Key," David finished for her.

The frequency of the rainy night. The frequency of the delivery room. The frequency of breakfast. They were all there. Inside that symbol.

"Anna," he whispered.

David's hands were trembling.

"Burn target," he heard his own voice, very steady. "All self-perception concerning 'David Keller'."

Who am I?

He stared at the face in the photo. No feeling at all. Like looking at a stranger's photo.

The ship moved forward. The sphere of light had condensed into a human form. Not a complete person, but a human outline.

He raised his own hand. The two hands, across that crevice, across 0.01 millimeters of distance, faced each other.

She once existed. And he remembers this.
